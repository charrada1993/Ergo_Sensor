Predictive Biomechanics Architecture

The Ergo Sensor AI Engine leverages a highly optimized machine learning pipeline to perform real-time ergonomic risk assessment and musculoskeletal disorder (MSD) classification. The system extracts 75 kinematic features—including raw joint angles, rolling means/standard deviations, frame-lagged histories, and dynamic joint accelerations—from 12 bilateral body joints captured at 30 FPS.

Our ensemble architecture combines LightGBM Regressors for continuous risk scoring (R²=0.9981), Gradient Boosting Classifiers for 18-class clinical diagnosis (99.60% accuracy), and Isolation Forests integrated with 5 localized joint anomaly detectors to instantly identify anomalous biomechanical stress points. All hyperparameters were rigorously tuned via Optuna utilizing a TimeSeriesSplit strategy to strictly prevent temporal data leakage.

Deep Dive Evaluation Suite

Model 1 — Continuous Risk Regressor

Optuna-optimized gradient boosting. R²=0.9981 · RMSE=0.0079 · MAE=0.0056. Residuals remain centered at zero confirming minimal bias.

Model 2 — 18-Class Condition Classifier

Accuracy=99.60% · F1=96.67%. Evaluates 18 distinct TMS pathologies. Visualizing LogLoss convergence, confusion matrix, ROC-AUC per class, and Precision-Recall curves.

Model 3 — Severity Categorization

Classifies risk into Low/Medium/High severity bands. Accuracy=96.95% · Macro-F1=0.9411.

Model 4 — Localized Joint Anomaly Classifiers

5 independent models monitoring Neck, Shoulder, Wrist, Trunk, and Elbow. Average F1 Score=0.9906.

Model 5 — Global IsolationForest

Unsupervised anomaly detection using 300 estimators (max_samples=256). Identifies complex multivariate outliers in kinematic chains.
